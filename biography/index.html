About Shina

Shina was born in Seoul, Korea in 1987. She learnt to play the clarinet from Hyun-gak Shin (Principal Clarinettist, KBS Philharmonic Orchestra in Seoul) at the age of 13.

In 2004, she moved to Vienna, Austria to pursue her musical career. She began studying the clarinet at the age of 16 at the University of Music and Performing Arts Vienna under Prof. Horst Hajek, who was the principal clarinettist of the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra. During her studies, she has gained her experiences by playing in orchestras and chamber music groups such as ORF radio symphony orchestra, Junge Philharmonie Orchestra, Webern Symphony Orchestra, Max Steiner Film music orchestra and Korean-Austrian Philharmonic Orchestra. She was a member of Tarqa Wind Quintet who was invited to the Carl Nielsen Chamber music Competition in 2015 and Tarqa was the semi-finalist. She has gained her experience as a chamber music player by playing Clarinet Trio, Quintet and Wind Quintet.

She graduated with a Bachelor degree in 2009 and a Master degree (Magistra Artium) in 2011 both with distinctions. After that, she began studying music education and choir conducting at the University of Music and Performing Arts under Wolfgang Kornberger and Alois Glaßner. She also studied piano under Maria Prinz.

2017, she moved to Edinburgh, United Kingdom. Shina Kim was the musical director of Bristo Concert Community Band in Edinburgh for a year. She is currently teaching clarinet, piano, recorder and music theory at Morningside Music School in Edinburgh since 2018.
She has been playing for various music projects such as the Fringe Festival and International Festival in Edinburgh and Glasgow. In early 2020, she has founded a wind quintet in Edinburgh with the musicians from Scottish Sinfonia.
